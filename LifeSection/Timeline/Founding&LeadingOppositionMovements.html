In the mid-1990s, Beyene Petros became actively involved in Ethiopia’s opposition politics.

Promoting Social Justice: The party sought to address the deep inequalities in Ethiopian society, particularly regarding access to education, healthcare, and economic opportunities.

Advocating for Federalism: The ESDP supported a genuine federal system that respected Ethiopia’s ethnic and cultural diversity while promoting national unity.

Defending Human Rights: The party emphasized the protection of civil liberties, freedom of speech, and political pluralism.

Recognizing the limitations of working within the EPRDF-dominated political system, he aligned himself with opposition parties that sought to challenge the government’s authoritarian tendencies and advocate for democratic reforms.

With a strong academic background and a commitment to social justice, Professor Beyene’s foray into politics was driven by a deep sense of responsibility to challenge authoritarianism, advocate for human rights, and promote equitable development.

In the mid-1990s, Professor Beyene co-founded the Ethiopian Social Democratic Party (ESDP),

marking his formal entry into organized politics.


The ESDP was established to address the growing discontent with the EPRDF’s governance and to advocate for a more inclusive, democratic Ethiopia.


Beyene’s role as a founder and leader of the party demonstrated his commitment to building

a political platform grounded in social justice, equality, and constitutional democracy.

The ESDP was founded on >a progressive and inclusive political philosophy.

Ethiopia’s opposition landscape in the early 2000s was highly fragmented, with numerous small parties representing different ethnic, ideological, and regional interests. This fragmentation weakened the opposition’s ability to challenge the EPRDF,
which had consolidated its power and controlled the country’s political and economic systems.
